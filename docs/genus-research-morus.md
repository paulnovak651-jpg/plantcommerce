# Genus Research: Morus (Mulberries)

## Genus Metadata

-   **Genus:** Morus\
-   **Common Name:** Mulberries\
-   **Family:** Moraceae

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Species: Morus alba (White Mulberry)

**Description:**\
Widely planted and naturalized across North America as a fast-growing
fruit and fodder tree; commonly used in permaculture systems for
abundant berries and poultry/wildlife feed. Can be invasive in parts of
the U.S. and hybridizes with native red mulberry.

### Growing Profile

-   USDA Zones: 4--8\
-   Soil Drainage: Well-drained\
-   Soil Textures: Loam, sandy loam, clay loam, sand, clay\
-   Sun: Full sun\
-   Growth Rate: Fast\
-   Mature Height: 30--60 ft\
-   Mature Spread: 30--50 ft\
-   Harvest Season: Early--Mid\
-   Native Range: Asia (introduced and naturalized in North America)

### Pollination

-   Wind pollinated\
-   Typically dioecious (male + female trees required for fruit)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Species: Morus rubra (Red Mulberry)

**Description:**\
Primary native mulberry of eastern North America; widely planted in food
forests and wildlife systems.

### Growing Profile

-   USDA Zones: 4--9\
-   Soil Drainage: Well-drained\
-   Soil Textures: Loam, sandy loam, clay loam, sand, clay\
-   Sun: Full sun\
-   Growth Rate: Fast\
-   Mature Height: 25--60 ft\
-   Mature Spread: 35--40 ft\
-   Harvest Season: Early--Mid\
-   Native Range: Eastern North America

### Pollination

-   Wind pollinated\
-   Typically dioecious

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Species: Morus microphylla (Texas Mulberry)

**Description:**\
Drought- and heat-adapted native mulberry suitable for dryland
permaculture systems in the U.S. Southwest.

### Growing Profile

-   Soil Drainage: Well-drained\
-   Soil Textures: Loam, sandy loam, clay loam, sand, clay\
-   Sun: Full sun\
-   Growth Rate: Moderate\
-   Mature Height: 20--30 ft\
-   Mature Spread: 20--30 ft\
-   Harvest Season: Early\
-   Native Range: Southwestern U.S. and northern Mexico

### Pollination

-   Wind pollinated\
-   Assume cross-pollination unless confirmed self-fertile

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Species: Morus nigra (Black Mulberry)

**Description:**\
High-flavor mulberry valued in warmer climates; less cold-hardy and less
widely planted than white or red mulberry.

### Growing Profile

-   USDA Zones: 5+ (best in warm climates)\
-   Soil Drainage: Well-drained\
-   Soil Textures: Loam, sandy loam, clay loam\
-   Sun: Full sun\
-   Growth Rate: Slow\
-   Harvest Season: Mid\
-   Native Range: Asia

### Pollination

-   Wind pollinated\
-   Pollination biology varies; ensure compatible pollinator unless
    confirmed self-fertile

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Species: Morus macroura (Pakistan Mulberry)

**Description:**\
Popular in rare-fruit and permaculture circles for very long, sweet
berries; best suited to warmer zones.

### Growing Profile

-   USDA Zones: 7--10\
-   Soil Drainage: Well-drained\
-   Soil Textures: Loam, sandy loam, clay loam\
-   Sun: Full sun\
-   Growth Rate: Fast\
-   Mature Height: 15--30 ft\
-   Years to Bearing: 2--3\
-   Harvest Season: Mid--Late\
-   Native Range: Asia (Himalayan region)

### Pollination

-   Commonly sold as self-fertile\
-   Wind pollinated

## Hybrid Group: Morus alba × Morus rubra

**Description:**\
Common "everbearing" mulberries in North American food forests (e.g.,
Illinois Everbearing, Silk Hope). Selected for long harvest windows and
heavy production.

-   Soil Drainage: Well-drained\
-   Soil Textures: Loam, sandy loam, clay loam, sand, clay\
-   Sun: Full sun\
-   Growth Rate: Fast\
-   Harvest Season: Early--Mid
